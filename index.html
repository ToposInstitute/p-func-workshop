Workshop on Polynomial Functors

At the Topos Institute and online via Zoom
15–19 March (UTC), 2021

Participants will learn background material and hear the latest progress on polynomial functors, monads, and comonads, their algebras and coalgebras, as well as on operads, parametric right adjoints, and cofunctors, with applications to combinatorics, database theory, dynamical systems, higher category theory, logic, and type theory.

Talks will be recorded for posterity.

(This may be the first in a series of such workshops.)

Speakers

  • Thorsten Altenkirch: Containers and inductive types
  • Steve Awodey: Polynomial functors and natural models of type theory
  • Michael Batanin: Grothendieck homotopy theory and polynomial monads
  • Bryce Clarke: Cofunctors, lenses, and split opfibrations
  • Marcelo Fiore: Stable species of structures
  • Richard Garner: Bicategories of polynomials
  • David Gepner: Analytic monads and ∞-operads
  • Helle Hvid Hansen: Coalgebraic modal logic: a tutorial
  • Rune Haugseng: Polynomial functors and Segal conditions
  • Bart Jacobs: lifting of polynomial functors for logical reasoning
  • André Joyal: Three kinds of polynomial functors
  • Fredrik Nordvall Forsberg
  • Kristina Sojakova: Initial algebras in homotopy type theory
  • David Spivak: The framed bicategory of polynomials: theory and applications
  • Ross Street: Polynomials as spans
  • Tarmo Uustalu

Organizers

Joachim Kock and David Spivak

Participation

Anybody interested in participating is welcome. If you would like to attend, please contact David Spivak or Joachim Kock. A zoom link will be sent out to registered participants a few days before the workshop.
